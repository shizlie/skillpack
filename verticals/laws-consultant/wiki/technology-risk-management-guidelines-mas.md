## Coverage in this ingestion

The guideline includes 15 sections and Annexes A-C, spanning governance, risk management framework, software development, IT service management, resilience, access control, cryptography, cybersecurity operations/assessment, online financial services, and IT audit. (source: Technology Risk Management Guidelines - Monetary Authority of Singapore.md)

## Key control themes captured

The guideline states it is principle-based guidance to promote sound technology risk practices, and implementation should be commensurate with the FI's risk and complexity; observance of the spirit of the guidance is considered in MAS supervision. (source: Technology Risk Management Guidelines - Monetary Authority of Singapore.md)

Board and senior management are expected to establish and review technology risk strategy, approve risk appetite/tolerance, resource accountable executives, and ensure independent audit coverage. (source: Technology Risk Management Guidelines - Monetary Authority of Singapore.md)

Third-party technology dependencies are explicitly addressed: FIs should assess confidentiality, integrity, and availability risks before entering partnerships/agreements and ensure ongoing diligence by providers. (source: Technology Risk Management Guidelines - Monetary Authority of Singapore.md)

Software controls emphasise secure SDLC, secure coding, source code review, application security testing, and DevSecOps integration for frequent and secure release practices. (source: Technology Risk Management Guidelines - Monetary Authority of Singapore.md)

IT service management controls include patch management with timelines commensurate to patch/system criticality, and incident management frameworks with escalation, evidence handling, communications, and customer updates. (source: Technology Risk Management Guidelines - Monetary Authority of Singapore.md)

Resilience controls include system availability design, redundancy/fault tolerance for high-availability systems, and explicit recovery time objective (RTO) and recovery point objective (RPO) setting aligned to business recovery priorities. (source: Technology Risk Management Guidelines - Monetary Authority of Singapore.md)

Cyber response and assurance controls include cyber incident response planning, adversarial attack simulation exercises, intelligence-based scenario design, and remediation tracking of identified issues. (source: Technology Risk Management Guidelines - Monetary Authority of Singapore.md)

For online financial services, the guideline includes multi-factor authentication at login, transaction signing for high-risk activities, and real-time fraud monitoring with suspicious transaction handling and customer notification expectations. (source: Technology Risk Management Guidelines - Monetary Authority of Singapore.md)
