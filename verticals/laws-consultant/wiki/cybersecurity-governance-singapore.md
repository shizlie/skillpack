# Cybersecurity Governance Singapore

**Summary**: Governance-oriented synthesis tying Cybersecurity Act institutional/sector framing, Computer Misuse Act offence framing, and MAS TRM governance and control expectations for financial institutions.  

**Sources**:
- Cybersecurity Act 2018 - Singapore Statutes Online.md
- Computer Misuse Act 1993 - Singapore Statutes Online.md
- Technology Risk Management Guidelines - Monetary Authority of Singapore.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-19

---

The Cybersecurity Act capture includes administrative structure (Part 2), CII obligations (Part 3), incident response (Part 4), and service-provider regulation (Part 5). (source: Cybersecurity Act 2018 - Singapore Statutes Online.md)

The Computer Misuse Act capture provides offence-related definitional foundations around unauthorised access/modification and misuse. (source: Computer Misuse Act 1993 - Singapore Statutes Online.md)

MAS TRM adds a board-and-senior-management governance layer for FIs, including explicit accountability for technology risk strategy, risk appetite alignment, and independent assurance through audit. (source: Technology Risk Management Guidelines - Monetary Authority of Singapore.md)

MAS TRM also extends governance into engineering and operations, including secure-by-design SDLC, DevSecOps, patch/incident processes, cyber exercises, and online financial service controls such as MFA and transaction signing. (source: Technology Risk Management Guidelines - Monetary Authority of Singapore.md)
